    Paper Presentation

    No matter what people tell you, words and ideas can change the world.

    But for good ideas to lead to great innovation and change, you need to present them to the people; make them argue and debate and finally bridge the gap between closed minds through careful dissection of ideas and solid presentation of facts.

    Topics:

    1. Lessons from the past: Are ongoing fiscal deficit crisis in different countries a living example of how we never learn from crises in the past?
    2. Role of banks and the monetary policy in perpetuating and propagating a crisis. What could have been done differently?
    3. 2008 crisis: an earthquake that took governments off guard? Or a creation of capitalism?

    General Rules:

    1. Participants are required to present paper on any one of the given topics which have been given on our facebook page in cohesion with the theme.
    2. Both UG & PG students are allowed, but a team should be formed entirely of UG students or PG students. Cross UG-PG teams are not allowed. UG & PG teams will be judged seperately.
    3. However, cross-college teams are allowed.
    4. Maximum no. of participants : 4

    The event has been divided into two rounds.

    Round 1: Selection for abstracts / proposals

    1. An abstract/ Proposal has to be mailed to us not exceeding 1000 words. The abstracts must contain an indicative bibliography.
    2. The abstract must throw light about what the objective of the paper is, and must contain a rough idea about how the paper has been planned out and the questions it aspires to answer.
    3. The abstracts must be submitted before the deadline to the following : email id: submissions@yesm.in with subject as: <name of the event>_<topic name>.
    4. The file name should be as follows Collegename(s).nameofanyoneoftheparticipants.
    5. The deadline for submission of the abstracts is 15th December, 2018.

    The selected candidates of this round will be notified via e-mail, as well as by our facebook page. The selected candidates are eligible for Round II, the details of which have been given below.

    Round 2: Submission of the main paper by selected candidates.

    1. The cover page must include the title of the paper, details of the participants (name, email id, mobile no.). It is mandatory to mention if the paper is to be treated as a UG or a PG entry.
    2. An abstract of the paper is mandatory. It must not exceed 500 words in the main paper. This should go on the second page. The entire paper should not exceed 5000 words approximately.
    3. Font: Times New Roman, 12 pt for normal writing, 16 pt for headings, 14 pt for subheadings and 10 pt for footnotes, Line spacing: 2.0.Bold and italic are accepted.
    4. All diagrams, tables must be self-drawn or taken from a data source. In the latter case, appropriate data source must be mentioned. Both primary and secondary data will be accepted.
    5. For citing other papers and/or articles, use APA style of referencing. For example: (Lucas, 1988) (Refer this)
    6. Add footnotes and side notes for all necessary details required. Remember to acknowledge all books / websites / other material consulted during the course of writing your research paper. Failure to do so will amount to plagiarism which is unacceptable.
